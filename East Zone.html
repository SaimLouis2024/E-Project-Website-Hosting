East Zone

Introduction

About East Zone:

There are multiple ways of dividing the planet, and one option is to divide the planet between east and west. Ultimately, which direction is east versus west is relative, but generally, the eastern hemisphere refers to all countries that are east of the prime meridian and west of the international dateline. Some of the most populated areas in the world are located in the eastern hemisphere. A few examples include Asia, most of Europe, and Africa. Therefore, there are a lot of countries that are contained in the eastern hemisphere. Lots of countries in Europe, such as the United Kingdom, the Netherlands, Belgium, France, Spain, and Germany are located in the eastern hemisphere. There are also a lot of countries in Asia that are located in the eastern hemisphere. Examples include Japan, China, South Korea, North Korea, Hong Kong, and Singapore. Most of the islands in Oceania are also located in the eastern hemisphere. Countries in Africa, such as Egypt, Sudan, the Congo, Nigeria, and Kenya are also located in the eastern hemisphere.

Ecological Features:

The East Zone, or Eastern Hemisphere, covers regions east of the Prime Meridian. It features:

Geography:

This zone encompasses much of Asia, Europe, Africa, and parts of Oceania. It includes diverse landscapes such as the Himalayas, the Gobi Desert, and the Mediterranean.

Climate:

The climate varies from temperate in Europe and parts of Asia to tropical in Southeast Asia and arid in North Africa.

Economy and Culture:

The Eastern Hemisphere hosts some of the world's largest and most influential economies, including China and India. It is culturally diverse, with a rich heritage spanning ancient civilizations to modern cultures.

Countries Located In East Zone:

The Eastern Hemisphere includes the continents of Asia, Africa, and most of Europe. Countries include China, Japan, India, the UK, Belgium, France, Germany, the Netherlands, Egypt, Sudan, North Korea, South Korea, and Australia.

Population Overview:

As of recent estimates, the Eastern Hemisphere is home to approximately 80% of the world's population. This includes densely populated regions such as Asia, which is the most populous continent, and diverse areas such as Africa and Europe.

Major Population Centers:

It is the cradle of many of the world's ancient civilizations and has been central to much of human history and development.

East Asia:

East Asia is the most populous continent, with over 4.7 billion people. Key population centers include:

  • China:

    The most populous country in the world with over 1.4 billion people. Urban areas like Beijing and Shanghai are major population hubs.

  • India:

    Close behind China in population size, with over 1.4 billion people. Major cities include Mumbai and Delhi.

  • South-East Asia:

    Countries such as Indonesia, the Philippines, and Vietnam have significant populations, with Jakarta and Manila being key urban centers.

Oceania Territory:

There are a variety of definitions of Oceania. The most plausible is to refer to the vast regions, island territories and adjacent seas in the Pacific Ocean, east of Maritime Southeast Asia and Australia and west of South America.In the vast area of Oceania, there are 14 independent countries and several dependent territories. The region consisting of Australia, New Zealand, New Guinea, and neighboring islands in the Pacific Ocean is sometimes referred to as Australasia.
Oceania covers an area of approximately 100 million square kilometers; this is about one-fifth of Earth's surface area. The water continent includes more than 10,000 islands. With a total land area (excluding Australia but including Papua New Guinea and New Zealand) of approximately 822,800 km² (317,700 sq mi), it is slightly larger than Turkey or somewhat smaller than half of Alaska. By far the largest country in Oceania in terms of area is Australia with 7,692,024 km², followed by Papua New Guinea with 462,840 km² and New Zealand with 270,467 km². The smallest independent country in Oceania is the island nation of Nauru, which covers an area of 21 km² (8 sq mi).
